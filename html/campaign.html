Time for Time's Sake

An ePaper smartwatch that lasts for days, not hours.
Minimal. Hackable. Open-source.

The Problem with Time

Smartwatches today are glorified notification buzzers that die faster than a mayfly in winter.

We're trapped in a cycle of daily charging, proprietary everything, and devices that become e-waste the moment the company decides to "innovate."

How we're fixing it?

The T1E is an ePaper smartwatch built with intention.

4+ days of battery life, completely open-source, and designed to be repaired, not replaced.

Core Philosophy

Time should be about living, not constantly charging. Your device should enhance your life, not become another thing you need to babysit.

What makes the T1E absurdly good?

4+

Days of Battery Life

Target: 16 days with partial refreshes. The ePaper display only uses power when updating, giving you weeks of timekeeping on a single charge.

How we did it:
  • • Optimized partial refresh algorithms
  • • Deep sleep power management
  • • Smart wake-up scheduling

ePaper Display

Crystal clear in direct sunlight, no glare, no eye strain. The display stays on without draining your battery - it's like magic, but it's just physics.

The science:
  • • Reflective display technology
  • • No backlight required
  • • Bistable pixel technology

100% Open Source

Hardware designs, firmware, CAD files - everything is yours. Fork it, modify it, improve it. No secrets, no lock-ins, no bullshit.

What you get:
  • • Complete schematics (KiCad)
  • • 3D printable case files
  • • Full firmware source code
  • • Assembly documentation
